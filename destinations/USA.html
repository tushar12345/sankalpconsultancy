Why study in USA?

United States of America is #1 choice of International Students because of its excellent academics, flexibility, funding options, world renowned faculty, infrastructure and research facilities. United States of America offers great campus experience; be it academics, sporting and research facilities. You will find most of the American university in top 500 University list that speaks about the quality of education and future career prospects students are getting after completion of their studies. United States of America offers warm welcome to the International students. As American society is multicultural and friendly, they give equal opportunity for study & work purpose.

Tuition fees and Living cost

United States of America, offers wide varities in terms of educational institutes and tuition fees. A student can expect to pay between 10,000 to 30,000 USD depending upon the type of Course and University they would like to pursue. Scholarship on merit and other funding facilities are accessible to deserving students, resulting in reduced Tuition fees & expenses. Students can even opt for various financial plans to pay the tuition fees. Students should prepare to spend between 700 to 1200 USD depending upon the location of the residence and amenities he/she would like to avail.

Work opportunities

Students are allowed to work part-time or full-time after approval of work authorization like CPT or OPT. They can even apply for H1B if they meet the criteria. Student can expect an annual salary between 60,000 to 90,000 USD after completion of their Graduate Studies.

Entry Requirement

  • Students who would like to study Bachelor, should prepare to write SAT, TOEFL or IELTS.
  • Students who would like to study Master of Science, should prepare to write GRE and TOEFL or IELTS.
  • Students who would like to study MBA, should prepare to write GMAT and TOEFL or IELTS.

Study in UK

The United Kingdom is a developed country and has the world's 5th largest economy by nominal GDP and 9th largest economy by purchasing power parity.
